CVW-8 Training Requirements

Below are the outlined training requirements. There are two types of certification you may hold: basic flight certification, and wingman certification. In order to be checked off on these certifications you must schedule a checkride with either the CAG or DCAG. These checkrides are not meant to be strict tests. You may always ask for help, and your instructor can walk you through certain steps. Once the instructor feels you are reasonably competent at the given task you may get it checked off. Instructors may also check you off during regular trainings if they see you demonstrate competency in a specific subject, even if a checkride was never scheduled.

Basic Flight Certification

This is a simple qualifcation to demonstrate airworthiness. To achieve this you must:

  • Demonstrate ability to start jet from cold and dark
  • Be able to communicate on the radios including:
    • Listening to and decyphering ATIS Airfield/Carrier communication
    • Inner and inter flight communication
  • Be proficient with land airfield operations including:
    • Taxiing to correct runway
    • Taking off
    • Flying the pattern
    • Landing
  • Be proficient with carrier operations including:
    • Taxiing and hooking up to catapult
    • Launching
    • CASE I, II, and III procedures

Wingman Certification

This certification qualifies you to participate in campaign missions. To achieve this you must:

  • Be familiar with basic navigation techniques such as:
    • TACAN
    • Manual waypoint entry
    • Heading bug and course lines
  • Be familiar with and be able to perform basic flight formations:
    • Parade
    • Cruise
    • Combat spread
  • Be able to operate the necessary systems and perform the necessary tasks that each mission will require (judged on a per mission basis)
